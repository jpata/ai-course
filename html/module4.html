Pattern 2: Natural Sciences & Digital Humanities

This module focuses on Computer Vision (CV). You will learn how to treat images as a rich source of research data, moving beyond manual observation to automated, large-scale analysis. We will cover the core patterns for finding, classifying, and extracting data from images, from microscope slides to historical archives.

Core Patterns & Models

Pattern

Object Detection ("Find and box it")

This pattern is used to find and draw bounding boxes around specific instances of objects. It doesn't just say "there is a car," it says "here is car 1, here is car 2," and provides the coordinates for each.

Key Research Applications:

  • Ecology: Automatically counting a specific species in camera trap footage.
  • Economics: Monitoring ports by counting ships or containers from satellite imagery.
  • Digital Humanities: Finding and cataloging specific objects (e.g., "hats") in archival photos.

Concrete Open Source Model: YOLOv8

Pattern

Segmentation ("Pixel-level classification")

Segmentation is far more granular than detection. It classifies every single pixel in an image, allowing you to find the precise shape and boundaries of an object.

Key Research Applications:

  • Computational Biology: Measuring tumor boundaries in medical MRIs or identifying individual cell nuclei.
  • Climate Science: Mapping land use (water vs. forest vs. urban) from satellite data.
  • Archaeology: Highlighting the precise outline of ancient structures or artifacts in ground-penetrating radar scans.

Concrete Open Source Model: SAM (Segment Anything Model)

Pattern

Optical Character Recognition (OCR)

OCR models are specialized to find and transcribe text from within an image. Modern models can handle varied fonts, handwriting, and challenging layouts.

Key Research Applications:

  • Digital Humanities: Digitizing entire archives of scanned newspapers, books, or manuscripts.
  • Social Sciences: Extracting data from thousands of scanned forms or surveys.
  • History: Transcribing text from archival photographs (e.g., shop signs, posters) to build a richer context.

Concrete Open Source Model: Tesseract / TrOCR

Module 4: Practical Task

Download the pre-trained YOLOv8 model. Write a Python script using the `ultralytics` library to run the model on a sample image (e.g., a picture of a busy street or a research-specific image) and draw bounding boxes on the detected objects (people, cars, cells, etc.).
