Course Modules

Module 1

The Open Source Research Toolkit

Learn to find, evaluate, and legally use open-source models for academic research from hubs like Hugging Face and GitHub.

Module 2

The Researcher's Methodology: Validation & Ethics

Treat AI as a scientific instrument. Learn to calibrate, validate, and manage bias to ensure reproducible, ethical results.

Module 3

Text Mining (Humanities & Social Sciences)

Analyze massive text archives, transcripts, or surveys for themes and entities using models like BERTopic and spaCy.

Module 4

Computer Vision (Natural Sciences & Digital Humanities)

Quantify and classify visual data, from microscope slides to satellite feeds, using models like YOLOv8 and the Segment Anything Model (SAM).
Module 5

Time-Series Analysis

Find patterns in non-visual data like sensor readings, climate records, and genetic sequences with models like ESMFold and Prophet.

Module 6

Multimodal AI

Solve complex problems by reasoning across data types, such as asking questions about charts or diagrams with models like LLaVA.

Module 7

Capstone: Designing a Research Pipeline

Synthesize all patterns to design and propose a unique, modular AI-based methodology for your own research question.
